picky disclaimers

How this works

We shuffle the player input and assign the "top of the deck" to one prize at a time. All data resides in your web browser; the data is never uploaded to the server. The implementation of the "Click here" button is entirely done on your computer.

Privacy Policy

This site does not use cookies or store data. Your data never leaves your browser. . It makes no difference because we don't keep your data. You're welcome?

Randomness

This version uses your browser's JavaScript's built-in Math.random() and the unfortunate realities of JavaScript IEEE-754 floating point. That's OK for trivial things but I wouldn't recommend it for running your state's lottery.

Specifically, we probably introduce a trivial bias by multiplying using the output of Math.random(); we have not verified the implementation of your browser's Math.random(); and the results are not publically verifyable or repeatable because we do not know the state of your browser's PRNG. So maybe you want a witness or something, I dunno.

The names are shuffled using a Fisher-Yates shuffle. This is known to be generally fair if the RNG is fair, unless there are bugs. A second random-sort shuffle implementation, helping to guard against bugs in the first shuffle. This is known to have a small bias, but the implementation is less likely to have bugs. Combined, they do a good [enough] job.

Ugliness

This is not a very attractive web site, or a very clever program. But it's not bad for a couple hours on a Saturday when I had forgotten JavaScript again, eh?
